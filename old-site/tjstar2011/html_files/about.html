FAQ

A few words about TjSTAR from Dr. Glazer
“I am excited about our symposium this year because it gives us an opportunity to celebrate our students’ research far beyond those who win Intel awards.  I want this symposium to be a success for our students – an opportunity for them to learn from one another, be inspired about opportunities at TJ, and interact with different role models across different sectors of society, including our alumni.  I want our students to observe professionals who tackle scientific and technological challenges and opportunities that impact different sectors of humanity.  You might also imagine how such a symposium can help TJ develop and strengthen partnerships with prospective mentors and donors, simply through their participation.”  

What will be the schedule for the day?
The TJSTAR will run all day on Thursday May 28, 2009 during regular school hours.  Students and faculty will be expected to report to TJ like it was every other day.  Student attendance will be taken at the beginning of the day, and in each session. 

The day will run similar to a conference, where students will sign-up for sessions ahead of time through an on-line scheduler similar to the way that they sign up for 8th period activities.  There will be a total of 4 concurrent sessions planned for 70 minutes, with common opening and culminating activities across the school.  As the date approaches and activities are confirmed, the exact times may be reduced to a little as 60-minutes, and perhaps up to 75 minutes.  The tentative, approximate session times are: 

8:30 – 8:50:     Students report to TA (or1st period) for overview, directions & schedules

9:00 – 10:10:   Session 1

10:10 – 10:20: Move

10:20 – 11:30: Session 2

11:30 – 12:15: Lunch

12:15 – 1:25:   Session 3

1:25 – 1:35:     Move

1:35 – 2:45:     Session 4

2:45 – 3:00:     Move/Break (this also gives us flexibility at this point to plan for additional time for the One Question activity for video or speakers.  TBD)

3:00 – 3:50:     One Question Kick-Off Activity (for 2009-2010).  Students will report to TA or designated locations TBD.

What kinds of activities will be offered throughout the day?

  • The following activities will be scheduled concurrently during the 4 sessions, in various rooms throughout the building:
      • IBET Research presentations
      • Senior Technology Research presentations
      • Students in grades 9 and 12 will be required to attend the session of their scheduled presentations; all students from all grades will be required to attend student research presentations for at least one session.  
  • The following activities are planned for each of the sessions, and may be offered in multiple locations simultaneously.  In the end, they may not be offered every session and will be dependent upon availability of guests and student interest:
      • Speakers and themed panels
      • Student workshops on research-related topics
      • Visits to Senior Technology Labs
      • Ethics in Research
      • Student Design Competitions
      • Science Competition Winners 
  • The following activities are planned as on-going “exhibits”           or “demonstrations” and will be available for viewing in a designated area of the building.  These are tentative ideas and will need confirmation based on availability:
      • Career Fair with TJ Alumni
      • Hands-on science activities
      • Cradle to Cradle grant winners
      • Science Competition Info
      • CIRPSICSR Projects
      • Club projects or demonstrations
      • Classroom projects or demonstration 

Where will the activities take place?
At various locations throughout the building.  Presentations will most likely be in trailers and classrooms.  Panels and workshops will need larger spaces.  Design competitions will take place in one of the gymnasiums or other large spaces.   Space will be tight!
 
What will staff be expected to do at TjSTAR?
All staff are expected to report to TJ for TjSTAR like any other day.  We will need staff to monitor students and activities throughout the day, but all teachers will have planning time and opportunities to visit the various presentations and activities scheduled throughout the day.  Some staff responsibilities will stem from committees, others will be needed for TjSTAR-specific issues. 

Who shall I contact if I have questions or ideas about TjSTAR?
Current TjSTAR Members are: Betsy Sandstrom, Koji Otani, Mary Stetson, Andrea Cobb, Heather Sondel, however many TJ faculty members are working on the details of the day through committees, by contacting partners and spreading the good word.  The committee meets first periods in Room 223.

Suggested Panel Topics

Science

  •  What is the future of creating genetically modified organisms? What is the future of cloning?
  • Will our current global economic structure increase the incidence of epidemics such as Avian flu?
  • What agricultural technology could be used to aid developing countries? How will this technology be used to help?
  • Discuss the relationship between business and scientific research and development.
  • How much money should the government contribute to fund research?
  • What is the government’s role in scientific research? How does the government prioritize funding?
  • How can capitalism and a healthy environment coexist?
  • What is the effect of role playing on someone’s psyche? Ex.: practice vs. learning
  • What role will space exploration play in the future? What should NASA’s role be in aerospace?
  • How does the increase in pharmaceutical drugs affect society?
  • What are the recent advances in medical technology and pharmaceutical research? 

Technology 

  • Should a government-sponsored specialized school offer classes in weapons technology?
  • How does technology affect people’s views on the world? How does science and technology affect development of human beings?
  • How can we use technology to predict economic downturns?
  • Discuss the economic implications of the bailout plan. 

Or ….. Suggest another topic of your own choosing!
